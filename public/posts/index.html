AI ethics is not an optimization problem

Often, AI researchers and engineers think of themselves as neutral and “objective”, operating in a framework of strict formalization. Fairness and absence of bias, however, are social constructs; there is no objectivity, no LaTeX-typesettable remedies, no algorithmic way out. AI models are developed based on a history and deployed in a context. In AI as in data science, the very absence of action can be of political significance. Why this post, why now When you work in a field as intellectually-satisfying, challenging and inspiring as software design for machine learning, it is easy to focus on the technical, keeping out of sight the broader context.
